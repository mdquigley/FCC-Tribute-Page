Members of Big Ups, from left to right: Joe Galarraga, Amar Lal, Brendan Finn, Carlos Salguero Jr.

A brief history of Big Ups:

  • 2009 Members of the band meet as undergraduate students in the Music Technology department at New York University.
  • 2010 They record a series of demos at the Steinhardt studios at NYU. It is mixed by guitarist Amar Lal and released via Bandcamp.com
  • 2011 The band releases a three track 7" vinyl record, Wake Up, recorded, mixed, and mastered by Paul Howells at Harvestworks in Manhattan, NY.
  • 2013 The band releases a 7" vinyl split with Flagland via East Coast Basements and Bedrooms, the psuedo-label/collective name used by Big Ups and peers to self-release music. The Big Ups half features two songs, "Fresh Meat" and "Wrong".
  • 2014 Recorded in 2013, Big Ups releases their debut full length album, 18 Hours Of Static in January of 2014. The album is recorded by Charles DeChants at Excello Recording in Brooklyn, NY, mixed by Amar Lal, and mastered by Sarah Register. It was released on Dead Labour in the US and Tough Love in the UK/Europe, and features album art by Austin Redwood.
  • 2014 Later in 2014, Big Ups releases a split 7" with Washer, via Exploding In Sound Records. The Big Ups half features tracks "Rash" and "Not Today". These was recorded at Thump in Brooklyn, engineered and mixed by Amar Lal and mastered by Sarah Register. A single 7" version was released in the UK by Tough Love.
  • 2016 After several years of touring the North America and the UK/Europe, the band releases their sophomore album, Before A Million Universes via Exploding In Sound Records in the US and Tough Love in the UK/Europe. It is recorded and mixed by Dan Frome at Frome Recording and mastered by Sarah Register. It features album art by Austin Redwood.
  • 2018 The band's third and final full length studio album is recorded at a number of studios in Brooklyn, including Thump, Moon Recording, Gravesend Recordings, and Crew Cuts, as well as the Moos Cabin, Ten Mile Lake, Cass County, MN. It was again mixed by Amar Lal and mastered by Sarah Register with album artwork by Austin Redwood. Titled Two Parts Together and released via Exploding In Sound Records, the album sees the band on a projection of growth and musical maturity. Expanding on and moving beyond the pop punk and hardcore idioms on which the band was founded. Later that year the band announces an indefinite hiatus as well as a string of final shows along the East Coast.
  • 2019 The band plays their last show at the Bowery Ballroom in Manhattan, NY alongside Bethlehem Steel, Washer, and Guerilla Toss, with an appearance by comedians Joe Pera and Dan Licata, as well as a unknown man named "Chappy".
